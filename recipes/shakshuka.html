    • Baked Beans Shakshuka

    A classic North African and Middle Eastern dish traditionally served at breakfast with a british twist, bring on the flavours!

    Description

    Shakshuka is a vibrant and hearty dish of poached eggs nestled in a rich, spiced tomato and pepper sauce. We add a british pantry staple in this Middle Eastern favorite, to make this the perfect accompaniment to some sasages for breakfast, or steak for a dinner to impress. Often served with crusty bread, it's perfect for breakfast, brunch, or even dinner, offering a delicious blend of savory and slightly tangy notes.

      Indgredients

    • 2 tbsp any oil
    • 1 x baked beans
    • 1 x kidney beans
    • 1 x chopped tinned tomatoes
    • 2 x large onion, diced
    • 2 x capsicum, diced
    • 3 garlic gloves, minced
    • 1 tbsp paprika
    • 1/4 tsp chili flakes (optional for heat)
    • feta cheese
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 4-6 large eggs
    • Crusty bread, for serving
    • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish and optional)

      Steps

    1. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and bell pepper, cooking for 5-7 minutes until softened.
    2. Add the garlic, paprika, and chili flakes (if using), cooking for 1 minute until fragrant.
    3. Add the baked beans and the kidney beans. Stir in the tomatoes (including juices) and sugar, then season with salt and pepper. Let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes until thickened.
    4. Make small wells in the tomato sauce and gently crack the eggs into the wells. Cover the skillet and let the eggs cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the whites are set and the yolks are still slightly runny.
    5. Add the feta and cover and let the eggs cook further and the feta cheese melt.
    6. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro and serve with crusty bread for dipping.
