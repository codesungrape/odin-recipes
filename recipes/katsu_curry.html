The Quick Japanese Katsu Curry

In this dish we air fry our breaded chicken steaks to save time!

Description

Japanese curry is a flavorful and mildly spiced dish featuring a rich, savory curry sauce made with onions, potatoes, and carrots. Typically served over rice and topped with breaded (katsu) chicken or pork, it's a comforting and hearty meal that's both satisfying and easy to make. The curry's thick, velvety texture and balance of sweet and savory flavors make it a beloved staple in Japanese cuisine.

    Indgredients (4 servings)

  • 2 tbsp, any oil
  • 2 x carrots, skinned and chopped 1cm wide peices
  • 1 x large onion, chopped and diced
  • 2 x medium size potatoes, skinned and cut into small cubes
  • 100g japanese curry paste
  • 540ml water
  • 4 x breaded chicken cutlet, to be put in airfryer as per instructions

    Steps

  1. Stir-fry vegetables with oil in a large skillet on medium heat for approx. 5 min.
  2. Add water and bring to boil. Reduce heat, cover and simmer until ingredients are tender, approx. 15min.
  3. Turn the heat off, break S&B Golden Curry Sauce Mix into pieces and add them to the skillet. Stir until sauce mixes are completely melted. Simmer approx. 5 min., stirring constantly.
  4. Serve hot over rice or noodles.
